## Who are the gender-diverse folks?

Gender-diverse folks are people who belong to a gender that is different from their assigned gender at birth. These individuals may identify as transgender, genderqueer, genderfluid, non-binary, agender, bigender, or any other gender identity that is not strictly male or female. They may also be members of the LGBTQIA+ community, people of color, or people of different religions, ethnicities, and cultures.

Gender-diverse people may have chosen to express their gender identity in ways that differ from traditional gender roles, such as wearing clothing traditionally associated with the opposite gender. They may or may not have taken hormones or had [gender-affirming surgery](https://www.hopkinsmedicine.org/health/wellness-and-prevention/preparing-for-gender-affirmation-surgery-ask-the-experts) in order to align their physical appearance with their gender identity.

## Struggles of gender-diverse people

For many, the struggles begin in childhood. Gender-diverse children are often subject to bullying, rejection, and exclusion from their peers. This can lead to feelings of isolation and a sense of not belonging. It can also contribute to [psychological issues](https://tobybarrontherapy.com/blog/emdr-for-anxiety/), such as depression and anxiety.

The struggles of gender-diversepeople can continue into adulthood. In the workplace, they can face discrimination, harassment, and even job loss. In their day-to-day lives, they may feel unable to be their true selves and express their gender identity. This can lead to [feelings of being misunderstood](https://tobybarrontherapy.com/blog/social-anxiety-as-lgbtq/), judged, and marginalized.

The difficulties faced by [gender nonconforming people](https://tobybarrontherapy.com/blog/what-does-non-binary-mean/) can be compounded if they are members of other marginalized communities. For example, people of color and those from low-income backgrounds may face additional barriers in accessing support and services and often face higher levels of discrimination and violence.

Gender nonconforming folks may be denied basic civil rights, such as the right to marry, due to their gender identity or expression. In many countries, transgenderpeople are not allowed to legally change their name or gender markers on identity documents.

Gender-diverse folks may experience a range of emotions due to their gender identity, including confusion, fear, guilt, and sadness. Many people who are nonconforming or gender diverse [struggle with self-acceptance](https://tobybarrontherapy.com/blog/therapy-for-depression/). This is often due to the lack of acceptance from family, friends, and society as a whole.

It is important to create safe and supportive environments where gender-diverse folks can openly express themselves, find support, and access resources to help them navigate their gender identity.

Gender-diverse folks should also be able to access the same health care and mental health services as everyone else. Unfortunately, many health care providers and mental health professionals are not properly trained to understand the needs of gender-diverse individuals and may not provide the best care and treatment.

Gender-diverse people may seek assistance from health care professionals for many reasons, including coming out to their family or employer, seeking legal gender recognition, coping with discrimination, or dealing with unchecked transphobia.
